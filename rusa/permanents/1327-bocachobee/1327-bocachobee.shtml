Ride Description

This ride offers a sample of all types of South Florida road riding. Starting northward on Hwy 441 out of suburban Boca Raton, the route then turns west on country highways through sugarcane fields. Not far after the control at Canal Point, the route uses the L.O.S.T. or Lake Okeechobee Scenic Trail from Port Myakka to Okeechobee. The trail up on the dyke is paved and closed to motorized vehicles. Views include cattle ranches and Lake Okechobee. Wind speed and direction become important on this exposed, elevated trail around the lake.

After turning around in Okechoobee, the ride heads east all the way to the coast, then south along the beach roads to Boca Raton. The inland portions of the ride were rural with few services. The coast is almost all developed residential areas including Jupiter Island. A few bridges are the only hills on the ride.
